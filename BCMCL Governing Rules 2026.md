2026 BCMCL GOVERNING RULES

MANAGEMENT COMMITTEE AND ELECTION OF OFFICERS

1.  The British Columbia Mainland Cricket Association  ( “BCMCA), also known as the
British Columbia Mainland Cricket League (the “League”) shall be managed by a
management committee comprising of Six Executive Officers and Nine General
Members. The Executive Officers include President, First Vice President, Second Vice
President, Honorary Treasurer, Honorary Secretary, Director-Junior women Cricket
development. Any reference in these governing rules to BCMCA shall include and mean
the League and vice-versa. The Election of the Management Committee will occur as
follows:

a)  The Executive Officers of the League shall be elected for a term of two years at

an Annual General Meeting by members of the League.

b)  In odd numbered years the positions of President and second Vice-President and

Director Junior and Women Cricket shall be filled.

c)  In even numbered years the positions of first Vice-President, Honorary Treasurer

and Honorary Secretary shall be filled.

d)  General Members of the Management Committee shall be elected for a one-year

term at a Meeting of Delegates held in November of each year.

MEMBERSHIP APPLICATION and PAYMENT

2.  Clubs must apply each year for membership in  the League. Applications must be
received by December 1st for consideration for the following season. The following will
apply to a cricket club seeking membership in the League:

a)  Renewal membership application from existing clubs for their current teams shall
be accompanied by a fee of one hundred dollars ($100.00) per team which will
be applied on account of the annual League assessment. In addition, clubs are
required to pay six hundred and fifty dollars ($650.00) per team as a partial
payment towards the cost of cricket balls for the following season.

b)  An application from an existing club wishing to add a new team shall be

accompanied by an application fee of Seven hundred fifty dollars ($750.00) per
team of which three hundred dollars ($300.00) will be non-refundable. Upon
acceptance one hundred ($100) per team will be applied on account of the
annual League assessment.   In addition, clubs are required to pay six hundred
and fifty dollars ($650.00) per team as a partial payment towards the cost of
cricket balls for the following season.

c)  Non-Refundable $300.00 for a new team to an existing club will be waived if such

entrance fee was paid within five (5) years and such team was subsequently
withdrawn or not approved.

d)  Application from a new club shall be accompanied by an entrance fee of Three
thousand, Five Hundred dollars ($3500.00) of which five hundred ($500) will be
non-refundable and one hundred dollars ($100.00) per team will be applied on
account of the annual League assessment and $750 per team. In addition, clubs
are required to pay six hundred and fifty dollars ($650.00) per team as a partial
payment towards the cost of cricket balls for the following season.

e)  As a general principle the League will give priority to existing clubs adding new

teams over new club applications.

f)  All applications for membership in the League shall be subject to approval by the

Management Committee. In the event of an application being rejected, the
entrance fee shall be returned – less non-refundable fees.

g)  Any new team from an existing member club accepted by the BCMCA will be

placed in the lowest division.

h)  All payments by Clubs shall be by DIRECT DEPOSIT and League will provide

bank account information prior to December 1st of each year.

i)

In evaluating an application for a new team shall be at the sole discretion of the
Management Committee. Such discretion shall be guided by Criteria for
Approving New Teams policy as amended from time to time.

3. All outstanding BCMCA assessments must be paid by the stated deadline, failing
which the club concerned will be penalized the following:

a)

Failure to pay invoice by the due date i.e. April 01, June 01, August 01 (“Due

Date”):  $100 fine per team

b)

In the event the failure continues seven days past the Due Date: $200 fine

per team and 2 point deduction for each registered team of the delinquent club for each
week past the Due Date.

4. If a club fails to pay, in full, their BCMCA assessment, each registered member of that
club will be jointly and severally responsible and will have to pay their share of that debt
before they are allowed to re-register for the next season.

FAIR USE OF PLAYERS

28. When a player or players of a specific club are moved between teams within the
club, the following shall apply:

a) When a player is sent down or picked to play in a lower division, they must play in
that lower division for a minimum of three (3) games and must miss three games
from the last higher division played in prior to going back up to play again in the
higher division. For a player to have played a game their name must be on the
official team list handed to the opposing captain and the Umpires.

Explanatory Notes:

1)  a player from Division 2 is sent down to play in Division 4, they must play
a minimum of 3 games in Division 4 before they are eligible to play in
Division 2 again as a player must play 3 games in the lower division and
must miss 3 games in the higher division last played in.

2)  a player can drop from a higher division to a lower division for one game

(For example: from 1st division to 2nd division) and then can be further

UMPIRING

33. Clubs are required to provide umpires to ensure that games are adjudicated by
neutral umpires. Clubs are expected to ensure that individuals performing umpiring
assignments have a reasonably good knowledge of the Laws of Cricket. Every
registered club with the BCMCA is required to have the following number of registered
members who are certified as Cricket Canada Level One umpires (“Umpires):

a) Clubs with one (1) or two (2) teams shall have one (1) Umpire;

b) Clubs with three (3) teams shall have two (2) Umpires;

c) Clubs with four (4) & five (5) teams shall have three (3) Umpires;

d) Clubs with six (6) teams shall have four (4) Umpires.

e) Any club that fails to have the minimum required number of Umpires at any
time during the season will be fined $400.00. If, at any time, an Umpire leaves a
club and the club falls below the minimum requirements set out above the club
will inform the BCMCA Secretary and the BCMCA in conjunction with the
VCUSA will arrange to have a replacement certified within thirty days. The Club
will not be penalized if good faith efforts have been made to replace the Umpire
who has left the club with another Umpire.

42. Fifty percent (50%) of the fine collected as a result of the default will be reimbursed

PROTEST AND DISCIPLINE

54.  The Management Committee shall appoint a Protest and Discipline Committee to
deal with protests and reports of misconduct. Said Committee shall consist of at least
four members who shall not represent the same clubs as those involved in the protest or
report of misconduct.

10

55. Any club within a division shall have the right to protest the result of any match in
that division within seven (7) days of same by making a written submission to the
BCMCA Secretary stating clearly the basis on which the protest is being lodged.
Similarly, clubs and/or umpires shall have the right to file reports of misconduct.

56. The BCMCA Secretary shall as soon as possible furnish a copy of such protest or
report of misconduct and members of the Protest and Discipline Committee and to the
BCMCA Secretary of the club(s) whose member(s) is/are the individual(s) against whom
the protest or report of misconduct has been lodged. They shall within four (4) days
provide a written reply to the BCMCA Secretary.

57. Upon receipt of such written submission, the Protest and Discipline Committee shall
within reasonable time will call a hearing, if deemed necessary, make its decision and
provide a written decision to the affected clubs and/or the individual

58. The notice period of all protests, reports of misconduct, written replies to it and/or
requests for hearings refer to the period by which the written submission must be
received by the BCMCA Secretary.

59. The protest fee shall be two hundred dollars ($200.00). In the event of the protest
being sustained, such fee shall be returned. If the protest fails, such fee shall be
forfeited to the BCMCA. Clubs will be billed for unsuccessful protests.

60. Should a club or individual wish to appeal a decision of the Protest and Disciplinary
Committee, they must within five (5) days of the receipt of the decision, submit a written
appeal to the BCMCA Secretary specifying the grounds upon which the appeal is based,
along with the appeal fee. The appeal will be forwarded to the Appeal Committee by the
BCMCA Secretary.

61. The appeal fee shall be two hundred dollars ($200.00).

62. The Appeal Committee shall consist of three (3) members of the Management
Committee who do not serve on the Protest and Discipline Committee. If the Appeal
Committee deems a hearing is necessary, the club/individual will be notified in writing
within five (5) days of the receipt of the request for an appeal. The appropriate party will
also be given the time, location and date on which the appeal will be heard by the
Appeal Committee.

63. Only ONE representative of the club and the individual concerned will be required at
the meeting.

64. The appealing club/individual will be notified of any action taken.  Decisions of the
Appeal Committee are final and are not reviewable by the Management Committee.

65.  A suspension is deemed to have been served once the team on which the
infraction/complaint occurred has played the number of games of the suspension.
Suspensions may be deferred at the discretion of the Protest and Discipline Committee
or the Appeal Committee.

Stats Sheet are not submitted to the BCMCA Statistician by 11:59pm the following

STANDINGS

74.  The standings of each division shall be determined in the following manner:

a) highest number of points in the division, but in the event of matches

scheduled not being played, by the percentage of net points gained in matches played.

b) The team with the highest number of wins;

c)  the team with the winning record in head to head matches (against each other)

during the season;

d) the team with the higher net run rate (see definition) over the regular season.

e) the toss of a coin

75. In the event of any two teams in any division except the Elite Division tying for the
championship at the close of the BCMCL season, such teams shall hold the
championship jointly.

Explanatory Notes:

1)  To calculate run rate, the total runs scored, is divided by the total number of overs
batted by a team. If any team is all out in less than its full quota of overs, the
calculation of its run rate shall be based on the full quota of overs to which it would
have been entitled and not on the number of overs in which the team was
dismissed.

2)  To calculate net run rate (NRR), record the total runs scored by a team, and divide it
by the total overs faced/allocated (as above) throughout ALL season/competition
matches. Then record the total runs scored against the team and divide it by the
total overs faced/allocated to the opposing teams. Finally subtract the total runs per
over for and runs per over against the team throughout the season. For D/L
matches, the adjusted par scores, targets & overs will be used. Protested and
awarded matches will not be considered for calculation of NRR.

SHIELD FINALS

76. The two top teams at the end of the year in all divisions except the Elite Division
shall play in a Shield Final.

77. Shield Finals will be played on a date specified by the Management Committee. If
the match is not played to a conclusion (i.e. Rainout, abandoned or ends in a tie), both
teams will be declared joint winners.

78. No player shall participate in a Shield Final unless they have played in at least five
(5) matches during the current season for the club that he will be representing in the
Shield Final. The five games must have been played for the team they are representing

canceled match is NOT considered as a played match.

ELITE DIVISION PLAY-OFF REGULATIONS

79. The champion of the Elite Division will be determined by the play-off system.

80. Each play-off match will be subject to the Elite regulations.

81. The top four teams in the Elite Division will qualify for the play-offs.

82. In the event of two or more teams having the same net percentage at the end of the
season, the placing will be determined by the following criteria, applied in the following
order:

a) Result of meeting between the teams during the season;

b) Net run rate;

c) The toss of a coin.

Net Percentage means the net points achieved after deduction of any penalty points, as
the total amount available to the team.

83. The play-off system will consist of the following based on Elite Division  standings:

Semi-Final 1: Team 1 vs. Team 4

Semi-Final 2: Team 2 vs. Team 3

Final: Winner of Semi-Final 1 v. Winner of Semi-Final 2

84. The Semi-Finals shall be played on a date specified by the Management Committee.
If a Semi-Final is rained out, abandoned, tied or otherwise not played to a conclusion
then the team with the highest standing in the division will be declared the winner.

85. The Final will be played on a date specified by the Management Committee. If the
Final cannot be started on the initial date, the Final will be played on a make-up date
provided in advance by the Management Committee. If the Final is commenced but
subsequently abandoned due to weather, a new match will be played on a make-up
date provided in advance by the Management Committee.

86. If the Final is not played to a conclusion on the make-up date or ends in a tie, the
team with the highest standing in the division will be declared the winner.

87. A play-off match is deemed abandoned in the same manner as a regular BCMCL

88. No player shall participate in the play-offs unless they have played at least 5

STANDARDS OF BEHAVIOUR

89. BCMCA is committed to the health, safety and well-being of all club members and
participants and is dedicated to providing a safe environment for participating in the
sport of cricket. It has zero tolerance for any form of discrimination, harassment,
physical violence, criminal activity, destruction of city or BCMCA property including
playing mats and verbal abuse.

90. Clubs are responsible managing their club members and players and for their
behavior on and off field

91. All complaints about on and off-field behavior seriously. BCMCA will handle
complaints based on the principles of procedural fairness, and ensure:

a) all complaints will be taken seriously;

b) for all complaints the club and/or club members (defendant) will be given full
details of what is being alleged against them and have the opportunity to respond to
those allegations;

c) irrelevant matters will not be taken into account; and

d) decisions will be unbiased;

Serious complaints or allegations in particular such as physical abuse or attempts of
physical abuse or criminal activity or destruction of city or BCMCA property will be
reported to the police and/or relevant government or municipal or city authority.

92. If allegations are proven, then BCMCA reserves the right to penalize the club and/or
its club member by way of fines, recovery of costs for repair of the damage as well as
suspension up to an including removal of club members.
